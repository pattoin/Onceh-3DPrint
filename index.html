What we do

Four services, one continuous thread from concept to production-ready.

CAD development

Solid and surface modelling in SolidWorks and Rhino, from quick subD investigations to production-fidelity geometry — parametric where it needs to be, freeform where it doesn't.

Precision surfacing

Complex, high-volume-ready surfacing work — the kind that goes into a shipped Cisco phone or headset — brought to your product's form.

Rapid 3D printing

Same-week physical iterations across FDM, resin (SLA) and nylon (SLS), matched to what each round of testing actually needs — fit, look, or function.

Prototyping & DFM handoff

Functional proofs-of-concept, fit and usability testing, and a clean manufacturing handoff package — tolerances, draft, wall thickness, the details a factory actually asks for.

How a project runs

A fixed sequence, because each stage depends on what the last one proved.

Most engagements run four rounds. Some need one. We scope it to what de-risks your next decision fastest.

01

Concept & CAD

We translate your sketch, reference, or rough spec into a working 3D model — fast enough to see it, precise enough to print it.

Deliverable — CAD file + reviewable renders
02

Print & iterate

Physical prints go out for review against form, fit and feel. Changes get folded back into the model the same day.

Deliverable — physical prototype, round 01–0n
03

Functional & fit testing

Once the form holds, we test what the product actually needs to do — mechanisms, tolerances, assembly, real-world handling.

Deliverable — tested prototype + findings
04

Manufacturing handoff

Final geometry gets cleaned up with draft angles, wall thickness and tolerances noted — ready to quote with a factory.

Deliverable — production-ready CAD package
